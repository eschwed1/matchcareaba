NJ Autism Insurance Law and Your Coverage Rights

New Jersey's Autism Insurance Reform Law (P.L. 2009, c. 30) requires all commercial insurance plans regulated in New Jersey to cover the diagnosis and treatment of autism spectrum disorder, including Applied Behavior Analysis. This is a strong, enforceable mandate that protects Paterson families on commercial plans.

For families on commercial insurance in Passaic County, covered plans include:

  • Horizon Blue Cross Blue Shield of New Jersey
  • Aetna and UnitedHealthcare
  • Cigna

For families enrolled in NJ FamilyCare Medicaid, ABA therapy is a covered benefit under each of the following managed care organizations serving Passaic County:

  • Horizon NJ Health
  • Amerigroup NJ
  • Wellcare of New Jersey
  • Aetna Better Health of New Jersey

NJ FamilyCare plan verified before referral. Because different NJ FamilyCare MCOs have different provider networks, we confirm which plan you are enrolled in and verify the specific provider contract before connecting you — not after.

Early Intervention and School-Age Transitions in Passaic County

If your child is under age 3, the NJ Early Intervention System (NJEIS) — operated through the Division of Developmental Disabilities — provides free evaluations and services for eligible children. The Passaic County Early Intervention coordinator handles referrals for families in the Paterson area.

At age 3, children transition out of Early Intervention and into school-based services. For Paterson families, this means the Paterson Public Schools Child Study Team conducts an evaluation and, if eligible, provides Preschool Disabilities services through the public school system.

ABA therapy through a private provider complements school-based services. Many families in Paterson access ABA therapy in the afternoon or on weekends, in addition to their child's school programming. Match Care ABA helps you find providers who work with your family's schedule.

What to Expect From ABA Therapy

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is the most evidence-based treatment for autism spectrum disorder in children. ABA therapy uses structured techniques to develop communication, social skills, adaptive behavior, and daily living skills, while reducing behaviors that interfere with learning.

In Passaic County, ABA therapy is delivered in several settings:

  • In-home ABA — a therapist comes to your home, which many Paterson families prefer for younger children or families with transportation challenges
  • Clinic-based ABA — structured therapy at a dedicated ABA center in or near Paterson
  • School-based ABA — some providers work alongside Paterson Public Schools IEP teams
  • Hybrid models — combinations of home and clinic based on the child's treatment goals and family schedule

The number of hours your child receives each week will depend on their individual assessment and the treatment plan developed by a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA). Match Care ABA connects you with providers who will conduct a thorough initial assessment before recommending a treatment plan.
