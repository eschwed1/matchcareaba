NY Medicaid and Insurance Coverage for ABA in Rochester

New York State law requires ABA coverage — whether you have Medicaid or commercial insurance. Under NY Mental Hygiene Law §13.09 and NY Insurance Law §3221, all Medicaid managed care plans and commercial health insurers in New York must cover ABA therapy when medically necessary for children with autism. In the Rochester and Monroe County area, major Medicaid managed care plans include Fidelis Care, Excellus BlueCross BlueShield, and MVP Health Care. Commercial insurers serving Rochester families include Excellus BCBS (which is the regional Blue Cross plan for upstate NY), Aetna, UnitedHealthcare, and Cigna. NY Early Intervention Services in Rochester

Early Intervention in Monroe County

NYS Early Intervention Program: Birth Through Age 2

For children under age 3 in Rochester, the New York State Early Intervention Program (EIP) provides free developmental services — including ABA therapy where appropriate — for eligible children. Monroe County's Early Intervention program is administered through the Monroe County Department of Public Health. Parents, pediatricians, and childcare providers can all make referrals.

At age 3, children transition from Early Intervention into the Committee on Preschool Special Education (CPSE) through their local school district (Rochester City School District, Brighton, Pittsford, Greece, Irondequoit, etc.). The CPSE evaluates whether ABA therapy should continue as a preschool-age related service.

Match Care ABA can help Rochester families understand and navigate both the EIP and the CPSE transition process in Monroe County.
