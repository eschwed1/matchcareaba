ABA Therapy for Children with Autism in North Carolina

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is the most evidence-based treatment available for children with autism spectrum disorder. In North Carolina, ABA is delivered by Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs) working with Registered Behavior Technicians (RBTs) in clinic, home, and school settings. Programs are always individualized — based on a comprehensive assessment of your child's unique strengths, challenges, and goals.

Research consistently shows that earlier intervention produces better outcomes. Getting on a waitlist while you are still in the evaluation process is one of the most important steps NC parents can take, as provider availability in high-demand areas like Charlotte and the Triangle can require planning ahead.

NC's Autism Insurance Coverage

North Carolina passed its autism insurance mandate in 2015, requiring all state-regulated commercial insurance plans to cover ABA therapy for children with autism. Major NC insurers — including Blue Cross Blue Shield of North Carolina, Cigna, Aetna, and UnitedHealthcare — are required to cover ABA. North Carolina Medicaid underwent a major transformation with NC Medicaid Managed Care and the launch of Tailored Plans in 2024, which provide specialized coverage for autism and intellectual disabilities. The NC Innovations Waiver also provides Medicaid funding for autism services for eligible individuals.

Finding ABA Therapy in NC: The Real Challenges

The Challenge

Urban-Rural Divide and Insurance Complexity

North Carolina's geography creates unequal access to ABA therapy. Families in Charlotte, Raleigh, Durham, and the Triangle generally have the most provider options, though even these areas face high demand and waitlists. Families in smaller cities like Asheville, Fayetteville, Wilmington, and High Point may find fewer local options, and rural communities face even greater challenges.

Navigating NC Medicaid's transition to managed care — with multiple plans like Carolina Complete Health, Healthy Blue, AmeriHealth Caritas, and WellCare NC — adds complexity for families relying on Medicaid. Knowing which providers accept which Medicaid plans is essential, and Match Care ABA helps families sort this out before making a connection.
