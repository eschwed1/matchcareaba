NJ Autism Insurance Law and Your Coverage Rights

New Jersey's Autism Insurance Reform Law (P.L. 2009, c. 30) requires all commercial insurance plans regulated in New Jersey to cover the diagnosis and treatment of autism spectrum disorder, including Applied Behavior Analysis. This is one of the strongest autism insurance mandates in the country.

For Hackensack families on commercial insurance, this means your plan is legally required to cover ABA therapy when medically necessary. Common commercial plans in Bergen County include:

  • Horizon Blue Cross Blue Shield of New Jersey (dominant in Bergen County)
  • Aetna and UnitedHealthcare
  • Cigna and Oxford Health Plans

For families enrolled in NJ FamilyCare Medicaid, ABA therapy is a covered benefit under the following managed care organizations:

  • Horizon NJ Health
  • Amerigroup NJ
  • Wellcare of New Jersey
  • Aetna Better Health of New Jersey

Not sure which plan you have? That's okay. When you submit your information to Match Care ABA, we help you identify your managed care organization and find a provider who is contracted with that specific plan — not just "NJ FamilyCare" in general.

Early Intervention and School-Age Transitions in Bergen County

If your child is under age 3, the NJ Early Intervention System (NJEIS) — operated through the Division of Developmental Disabilities — provides evaluation and services at no cost to families. The Bergen County Early Intervention coordinator handles referrals for families in the Hackensack area.

At age 3, children transition out of Early Intervention and into services coordinated by the local school district. For Hackensack families, this means the Hackensack City School District's Child Study Team will conduct an evaluation and, if eligible, provide Preschool Disabilities services through the public school system.

ABA therapy through a private provider can complement school-based services and continue outside school hours. Match Care ABA helps families navigate both systems — connecting you with clinic-based or in-home ABA that works alongside your child's IEP.

What to Expect From ABA Therapy

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is the most evidence-based intervention for autism spectrum disorder in children. ABA therapy uses structured techniques to build communication, social, adaptive, and daily living skills while reducing behaviors that interfere with learning and daily life.

In Bergen County, ABA therapy is delivered in several settings:

  • Clinic-based ABA — structured therapy at a dedicated ABA center, common in the Hackensack and Paramus areas
  • In-home ABA — therapy delivered in the child's home, which many families prefer for young children
  • School-based or community ABA — some providers work alongside IEP teams in school settings
  • Hybrid models — combinations of clinic, home, and school settings based on treatment goals

Hours of ABA therapy vary by child. Children with more intensive needs may receive 20–40 hours per week; children with mild-to-moderate needs may receive 10–20 hours. Your child's BCBA will develop an individualized treatment plan after an initial assessment.
