The Landscape

A Military Community with Unique Insurance Needs

Fayetteville's ABA therapy landscape is shaped heavily by the presence of Fort Liberty. Many families carry TRICARE coverage — either TRICARE Prime or TRICARE Select — which covers ABA therapy but requires providers to be TRICARE-authorized. Not all ABA practices in the Fayetteville area have completed TRICARE authorization, so finding a verified TRICARE-accepting provider is the critical first step.

Cumberland County also has families relying on NC Medicaid Tailored Plans and Standard Plans. Match Care ABA confirms both TRICARE authorization status and NC Medicaid managed care plan acceptance before connecting you with any provider.

TRICARE Coverage for ABA Therapy

TRICARE covers ABA therapy for military families with a child who has an autism spectrum disorder diagnosis. Both TRICARE Prime and TRICARE Select cover ABA therapy when provided by a TRICARE-authorized ABA provider. NC General Statute 58-3-57 also requires commercial insurance plans to cover ABA, and NC Medicaid covers ABA through its managed care organizations. Match Care ABA verifies TRICARE authorization and Medicaid plan acceptance for every provider we connect you with.

NC Early Intervention Services in Fayetteville

NC System Info

NC Infant-Toddler Program and Cumberland County Schools

North Carolina's Infant-Toddler Program (ITP) provides free early intervention for Fayetteville children birth through age 2 with developmental delays. Cumberland County's ITP coordinator handles referrals and service coordination. At age 3, children transition to Cumberland County Schools for Preschool Exceptional Children services.

For military families, Exceptional Family Member Program (EFMP) enrollment provides additional coordination of special education and therapy services across PCS moves. Private ABA therapy through TRICARE can begin independently of and alongside these public services.
