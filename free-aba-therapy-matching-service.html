Free ABA Therapy Matching — Because Every Family Deserves Better Than a Waitlist

By Match Care ABA Team 8 min read Updated March 2026

Finding ABA therapy shouldn't feel like a second job. Match Care ABA is a free matching service that does the hard work for you — verifying providers, confirming insurance acceptance, and connecting your family directly with ABA therapy providers who are actually taking new clients. No cold calls. No dead ends. No guessing.

Why We Built This Service

If you've tried to find ABA therapy on your own, you already know how it goes. You search online, compile a list of providers in your area, and start making calls. Provider one doesn't answer. Provider two has a 12-month waitlist. Provider three doesn't accept your insurance — a fact you only learn after driving across town for an intake appointment. Provider four offers services, but only in a clinic 45 minutes away. You start over.

The Problem We Solve

ABA Therapy Is Hard to Find. That Needs to Change.

This is the reality for most families seeking ABA therapy in the United States. ABA therapy waitlists average 6 to 12 months in high-demand metro areas. In some regions, families wait longer. Meanwhile, the research is unambiguous: early intervention produces the best outcomes. Every month a child waits for therapy is a month of opportunity that cannot be recovered.

The families who get connected to services quickly are often the ones who know the system, have time to make dozens of phone calls, and have the resources to navigate a fragmented and opaque market. That is not fair. Every family — regardless of income, familiarity with the autism therapy system, or ability to spend hours on hold — deserves access to the information they need to get their child help.

Match Care ABA was built to change that. We believe the barrier between a family and the therapy their child needs should be information and access — not confusion, phone trees, and outdated provider directories.

What "Free" Actually Means

Match Care ABA is completely free for families. Always.

We are funded by ABA therapy providers who pay to be included in our verified network. Providers value the connection to families who are ready to enroll, whose insurance has already been confirmed, and whose geographic and service-type needs match the provider's availability. That value is what makes our model work — and it means you will never be charged for our matching service, asked for a credit card, or surprised by a hidden fee at any point in the process.

We are transparent about this because we believe you deserve to know exactly how the service works. Our incentive is to make the best possible match for your family — because happy families and successful matches are what keep providers engaged with our network.

How the Matching Service Works

The process is simple. You share a little information about your child and family, and we do the rest.

1
Step One

Tell Us About Your Family

Fill out our short intake form — it takes about three minutes. We ask for the basics: your child's age, your general location, any existing diagnosis or developmental concerns, your insurance plan, and what type of ABA therapy setting you are looking for (clinic, home, school, or a combination).

You do not need a diagnosis to get started. Many families come to us with developmental concerns but no formal diagnosis yet — and we can still help connect you with providers who accept referrals at that stage.

2
Step Two

We Do the Work

Once we have your information, our team gets to work. We cross-reference your family's needs against our verified provider network — checking real-time availability, confirming that providers currently accept new clients (not just in theory, but actually), and verifying that your specific insurance plan is accepted.

We also match on service type, location, and any specific circumstances your family has shared. The goal is a match that works — not just a name and phone number to add to your list.

  • Current enrollment availability confirmed directly with the provider
  • Insurance verification for your specific plan, not just "most insurances"
  • Location matching within a practical distance for your family
  • Service type alignment (clinic, home-based, school-based)
  • BCBA supervision verified
3
Step Three

Connect with Your Match

When we have a confirmed match, the provider reaches out to your family directly to schedule an intake consultation. No middleman. No repeated re-telling of your child's story to multiple layers of staff. You go straight to a provider who is ready to meet you.

If for any reason the initial match doesn't work out — the timing changes, your family's needs shift, or it just isn't the right fit — contact us and we will find alternatives. Our relationship with your family doesn't end at the first referral.

What We Verify Before Making a Match

Not all "provider directories" are created equal. Many list providers who haven't updated their availability in months — or years. Match Care ABA verifies the things that actually matter to your family before any referral is made.

  • Currently accepting new clients — Not just listed as "open" on paper, but verified to have actual availability for new enrollments in a reasonable timeframe.
  • Accepts your specific insurance plan — We verify in-network status for your particular carrier and plan, not just generic insurance acceptance.
  • Licensed and BCBA-supervised — All matched providers employ Board Certified Behavior Analysts who design and oversee treatment programs. We do not refer to programs without qualified BCBA oversight.
  • Located within a practical distance — We consider realistic travel time and commute, not just straight-line geography.
  • Offers the service type your family needs — Whether you need clinic-based, home-based, or school-based ABA, we match to providers who offer the specific setting that works for your child and schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs

Your Questions, Answered

Do I need a formal autism diagnosis to use the service?

No. Many families come to us with developmental concerns but no formal diagnosis yet. While some providers do require a diagnosis before starting ABA therapy, others accept referrals based on developmental concerns alone — particularly for early intervention. We will match your family with providers who are appropriate for your specific situation.

What age children do you serve?

We serve families of children of all ages. The majority of ABA therapy providers in our network serve children ages 2 through 21, though individual provider age ranges vary. Some providers specialize in early intervention (ages 2–5), others in school-age children, and some serve adolescents and young adults. We match based on your child's age and the provider's specific focus.

How long does matching take?

We aim to provide match results within 2–5 business days of receiving your completed intake form. In some cases — particularly in high-demand areas or for families with specialized insurance plans — it may take a few additional days to confirm availability. We will always keep you updated on where we are in the process.

What if I don't like the match or it doesn't work out?

Contact us and we will look for alternative providers. Our goal is to find you a connection that actually works — not just to close a referral. If circumstances change or the initial match isn't the right fit for your family, we want to hear about it so we can help find a better option.

Is this really free? What's the catch?

It is genuinely free for families. Match Care ABA is funded by providers in our network who pay to receive qualified, pre-verified referrals. You will never be charged, asked for payment information, or surprised by a fee at any stage of the process.
