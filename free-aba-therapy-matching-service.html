Free ABA Therapy Matching Service for Families of Children With Autism

By The Match Care ABA Team 6 min read Updated July 2026

Match Care ABA is a free ABA therapy matching service for families of children with autism. We connect you with verified, currently-accepting ABA therapy providers who take your insurance, at no cost to your family, ever. You fill out a short form, and our team handles the searching, the phone calls, and the insurance verification for you. Most families receive a provider match within a few days, not the months of waitlists and dead-end calls that the typical search involves. The service is available in New York, New Jersey, North Carolina, Colorado, Washington D.C., Georgia, Indiana, Maryland, Mississippi, and Virginia.

What Is a Free ABA Matching Service and How Does It Work?

A free ABA matching service takes the hardest parts of finding therapy off your plate. Instead of spending weeks cold-calling providers from an outdated directory, you share some basic information about your child and your family's needs, and a team does the searching, calling, and verifying for you.

Here is how Match Care ABA works:

  1. You fill out a short intake form, about 3 minutes. We ask about your child's age, your location, your insurance plan, and whether you prefer home-based, clinic-based, or school-based ABA therapy.
  2. Our team searches our provider network, checking real-time availability, confirming your specific insurance is accepted, and verifying that a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) supervises all care.
  3. We reach out to you with your match, and you connect with the provider when you are ready to schedule.

How Match Care ABA Matches You in Days, Not Months

The average ABA therapy waitlist in the United States runs 6 to 12 months. For many families, that means half a year or more of missed early-intervention time while their child sits on a list.

Match Care ABA changes that timeline. Because we maintain direct relationships with providers across our service area and verify their availability before making a match, most families hear back within a matter of days, depending on their area and insurance.

We move quickly because we do the homework that would otherwise take you weeks of phone calls:

  • We confirm that a provider is actually taking new clients right now, not just listed as "accepting patients" on a website that hasn't been updated in a year
  • We verify that your specific insurance plan is in-network, so you don't waste time on providers who can't bill your carrier
  • We match on location and service type so the connection makes practical sense for your daily life

Why speed matters: Research consistently shows that earlier access to ABA therapy leads to stronger developmental outcomes. Every week spent searching is a week your child could be making progress. Match Care ABA was built to close that gap.

What Insurance Plans Are Covered?

Match Care ABA works with families on virtually every major insurance plan, including:

  • Commercial plans: Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Anthem, Humana, and more
  • Medicaid: We serve families enrolled in state Medicaid programs and help navigate managed care plan networks
  • Employer-sponsored and marketplace plans: If your plan covers ABA therapy, we can match you

We verify your specific plan's in-network status with each provider before making a match, so you know it will work before anyone picks up the phone.

How to Get Started

Common Questions From Families

Is Match Care ABA really free for families?

Yes, Match Care ABA is completely free for families. You are never charged a fee, asked for payment information, or surprised by any hidden cost at any point in the process.

How fast will I get matched with an ABA provider?

Most families receive a provider match within a matter of days, depending on your area and insurance. We keep you updated throughout the process.

What if my insurance isn't accepted by any providers?

We work with providers who accept a wide range of insurance plans, including Medicaid. If your specific plan presents a challenge, we will let you know and help you explore alternatives, including providers who may offer out-of-network options.
