NC Autism Insurance Law and Your Coverage Rights

North Carolina General Statute 58-3-57 requires all state-regulated commercial insurance plans to cover ABA therapy when medically necessary for autism spectrum disorder. This applies to BCBS NC, Aetna, UnitedHealthcare, and other commercial plans carried by Wilmington's civilian and retiree population.

For commercial insurance families in New Hanover County, covered plans include:

  • Blue Cross Blue Shield of North Carolina
  • Aetna and UnitedHealthcare

For NC Medicaid families in the coastal region, ABA therapy is covered through managed care organizations including:

  • Healthy Blue
  • AmeriHealth Caritas NC (serving the coastal NC region)

For military families, TRICARE covers ABA therapy for beneficiaries with an autism spectrum disorder diagnosis. Both TRICARE Prime and TRICARE Select cover ABA, but families must use providers enrolled in TRICARE's ABA provider network. Early Intervention and School-Age Transitions in New Hanover County

If your child is under age 3, the NC Infant-Toddler Program (ITP) provides free evaluations and early intervention services for eligible children in New Hanover County. Families can self-refer or be referred by a physician. For military families, NC ITP services are available regardless of where the family is stationed — eligibility is based on the child's residence in NC.

At age 3, children transition from NC ITP into services coordinated by the local school system. For Wilmington families, this means New Hanover County Schools handles the transition and provides Preschool Exceptional Children (EC) services for eligible children.

Private ABA therapy complements school-based EC services. What to Expect From ABA Therapy

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is the most evidence-based treatment for autism spectrum disorder in children. ABA uses individualized, structured techniques to build communication, social skills, adaptive behavior, and daily living skills, and to support reduction of behaviors that interfere with learning and daily life.

In the Wilmington and coastal NC area, ABA therapy is delivered in several settings:

  • Clinic-based ABA — structured therapy at a dedicated ABA center in the Wilmington area
  • In-home ABA — a therapist comes to your home, preferred by many families and particularly convenient for military families with variable schedules
  • School-adjacent ABA — some providers coordinate with New Hanover County Schools EC teams
  • Hybrid models — combinations of clinic and home based on the child's individual treatment goals and family needs

A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) will conduct a comprehensive assessment before recommending a treatment plan. Under TRICARE, ABA therapy must be recommended by a physician and authorized by TRICARE before services begin. Match Care ABA connects you with providers familiar with TRICARE's authorization process so you are not caught off guard by administrative requirements.
