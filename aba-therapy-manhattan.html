Insurance Coverage for ABA in Manhattan

New York State law requires all insurance plans to cover ABA therapy for children with autism — including Medicaid Managed Care plans serving Manhattan families (HealthFirst, MetroPlus, Fidelis Care, Affinity Health Plan, Molina Healthcare), Child Health Plus, and commercial insurance. If your child has Medicaid through NYC HRA, they are covered for ABA therapy. Match Care ABA confirms which specific plans each provider accepts before connecting you.

Early Intervention and School-Age Services in Manhattan

NYC System Info

NYC Early Intervention, CPSE, and District 75

Manhattan families with children under 3 can access NYC's Early Intervention program, which provides developmental services at no cost. At age 3, children transition to the Committee on Preschool Special Education (CPSE), which can authorize services including ABA-based programming at 4410 approved preschools throughout the city.

School-age children with autism in Manhattan may be served by District 75 — NYC's specialized citywide school district — or within their local school through an Individualized Education Program (IEP). Private ABA therapy through insurance can run alongside school-based services and is often recommended by BCBAs and school teams working together.
